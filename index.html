AI On-ramp for Curious Developers

üìÖ 25 Feb 2025 ‚Ä¢ Continuing Education

Artificial Intelligence has transformed from a niche academic field to a mainstream technology that powers everything from our smartphones to enterprise software. As developers, understanding AI is no longer optional‚Äîit's becoming an essential part of our toolkit.

My First Steps into AI

My journey began with simple Python scripts using scikit-learn. I started with basic classification problems‚Äîpredicting housing prices, identifying spam emails, and recognizing handwritten digits. The magic of seeing a computer learn patterns from data was intoxicating.

Key Resources That Helped Me

Here are the resources that helped me most in my AI learning journey:

  • Fast.ai's Practical Deep Learning course
  • Andrew Ng's Machine Learning course on Coursera
  • Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras, and TensorFlow
  • Kaggle competitions for practical experience

Where to Start as a Developer

For developers looking to get started with AI, I recommend:

  1. Learn Python fundamentals if you haven't already
  2. Start with scikit-learn for traditional machine learning
  3. Move to TensorFlow or PyTorch for deep learning
  4. Build small projects to apply what you learn
  5. Contribute to open source AI projects

Finding Work When the Job Market is Bad

üìÖ 25 Jan 2025 ‚Ä¢ General

The tech job market can be volatile, with periods of intense hiring followed by sudden freezes. When I found myself looking for work during one of these downturns, I had to get creative with my approach.

Expanding My Skill Set

Instead of just applying for jobs, I used the time to:

  • Learn new technologies that were in demand
  • Build portfolio projects that solved real problems
  • Contribute to open source to gain visibility
  • Network through virtual meetups and conferences

Alternative Opportunities

I explored non-traditional paths:

  • Freelancing platforms to build experience
  • Startup incubators looking for technical co-founders
  • Non-tech companies needing digital transformation
  • Teaching programming to create income streams

Key Takeaways

The experience taught me:

  1. Your network is your most valuable asset
  2. Continuous learning keeps you competitive
  3. Sometimes the best opportunities come from unexpected places
  4. Persistence pays off‚Äîkeep improving and applying
